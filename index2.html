About Me

Applied mathematician & data scientist bridging theory and practice

I work at the intersection of applied mathematics, statistical learning, and industry analytics, developing models that inform strategic decisions and deepen our understanding of complex systems.

Short biography

I am a research scientist and data scientist based in Saskatoon, SK. My academic work focuses on turbulent flows, hybrid RANS–LES methods, and continuous eddy simulation (CES), while my industry work spans predictive analytics, risk modeling, and business intelligence for gaming and financial services.

I enjoy turning complex mathematics and large‑scale simulations into clear narratives that decision‑makers can act on—whether optimizing capital projects, improving marketing ROI, or designing more efficient engineering systems.
